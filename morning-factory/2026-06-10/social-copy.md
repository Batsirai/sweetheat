## LINKEDIN POST

**The Wound That Doesn't Look Like One**

I've worked with enough people — and reflected enough on my own childhood — to notice a pattern.

The highest achievers in any room are often the people who never quite believed they were enough. They built careers on proving it. Relationships on earning it. They're capable beyond measure and quietly, persistently afraid.

Not because anything terrible happened to them. Because nothing was ever *named*.

There's a concept in developmental psychology called "conditional regard" — when a child learns that warmth and approval arrive after performance, not before. Nobody teaches this intentionally. But children are extraordinary learners. They read the room. They connect the dots. And they carry those dots into adulthood.

Research on secure attachment is unambiguous: children who grow up with a clear, spoken, unconditional message — *you are loved, you belong here, nothing you do changes that* — regulate emotion better, form healthier relationships, and take better risks. Not because they were coddled. Because they didn't have to use all their energy wondering.

This Father's Day, I've been thinking about the simplest intervention available to any parent:

Say the true thing out loud. Regularly. Until it lands.

"You don't have to earn my love. You already have it. You always have."

Four seconds. A lifetime of scaffolding.

AlreadyLoved builds personalized children's books that do exactly this — weave a child's name into a story about who they already are. Not who they might become. Who they are right now.

Because the world will teach them to earn love if we don't get there first.
