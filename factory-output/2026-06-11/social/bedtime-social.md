## LinkedIn Post

Most discussions about bedtime routines focus on sleep hygiene.

What rarely gets discussed: the thirty seconds after the light goes out.

That's when the noise stops. That's when a child is still enough to really hear what the people who love them are saying.

And what gets said in that window doesn't just settle a child down. It shapes their internal working model — the story they tell themselves about who they are, what they're worth, and whether they belong.

We spend enormous effort on the mechanics of bedtime (the routine, the white noise, the consistent timing). But the most formative part of the whole ritual is the words spoken in the dark.

There's a distinction worth drawing here: **feedback** vs. **formation.**

Feedback: "You were really patient at dinner tonight."
Formation: "I see how you hold yourself when things get frustrating. You've always had that in you."

Feedback adjusts behavior. Formation builds identity.

The brands and books that understand this are the ones that leave something lasting.
