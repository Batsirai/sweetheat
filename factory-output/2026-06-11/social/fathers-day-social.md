## LinkedIn Post

There's a piece of research I keep coming back to.

Children don't build a sense of identity from strangers. They build it from the people they belong to — from the parents who know their whole name and use it with love.

This matters enormously for how we think about early childhood development. And, honestly, for how we think about what brands are for.

AlreadyLoved is a personalized children's book brand built on a single insight: **identity precedes behavior.** A child who knows they're already loved doesn't need to earn belonging — so they're free to grow, explore, and show up fully.

With Father's Day four days away, we've been thinking about the specific role fathers play in this. Research from the Fatherhood Institute is clear: how a dad *names* his child — the specific language he uses, the stories he tells about who they are — shapes that child's self-concept for decades.

Not praise. Not rewards. Language.

"You're the kid who notices things." "You're someone who gets back up." "You are already exactly who you're supposed to be."

That's the kind of language worth building into a Father's Day gift.

One question I'd love to hear your thoughts on: what do you think matters most in how we form identity in young children?
