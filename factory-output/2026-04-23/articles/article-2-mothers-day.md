## Why Most Mother's Day Gifts Don't Last

Here is the honest truth about most gifts: they're for the giver.

Not in a bad way. Flowers say *I thought of you.* A nice dinner says *you deserve a break.* A candle says *I know you like these.*

All of that is real and it matters.

But the gifts that last — the ones that get kept and talked about years later — are almost always the ones that said something *true* about someone. That named something the giver had been paying attention to.

A photo of a specific moment. A letter that said what was actually felt. A book that told a child who they were, in their own story, so the mother could hear it read back to her a hundred times over.

Those gifts don't fade because they were never really about the occasion. They were about the person.

## What Mothers Actually Want

Ask most mothers what they want — really ask them, like you mean it — and you'll hear some version of the same thing.

*I want to know I'm doing it right.*  
*I want to know my kids are going to be okay.*  
*I want to know that what I'm pouring into them is landing somewhere.*

Mother's Day can feel complicated for a lot of moms. Especially new moms. Especially moms in hard seasons. There's this unspoken weight of: *am I enough?*

The best gift you can give a mother is the one that answers that question.

Not with words to her. With words *to her child.*
