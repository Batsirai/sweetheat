## Phase 2: Article Written — 1

### "What Your Child Is Really Asking for This Summer"

**File:** `factory-output/2026-05-24/article-summer-belonging.md`
**Format:** Blog (engagement + brand voice)
**Word count:** ~1,600 words
**Publish by:** This week (Memorial Day weekend is the perfect launch window)

**Structure:**
- Hook: Leo on the back steps, last day of school — slightly unmoored. "He doesn't know what he is right now."
- Core insight: School doesn't just teach reading and math. It teaches children where they belong. Summer removes all of that. Nervous system starts looking for those signals at home.
- Named inner experience: "Am I somebody here?" — what kids are actually asking all summer
- What common summer behaviors mean underneath: "I'm bored," "watch me," "what are we doing today?"
- The case for slow hours: Boredom is where a child meets themselves. Don't rescue too quickly.
- Four things that answer the question: name them without reason, let them be boring with you, reflect who they are, answer what they're actually asking
- Closing: Emma's mom and the rock store. Imperfect moments that stay.
- Soft CTA: AlreadyLoved personalized books carry belonging into every season

**Quality gate:**
- ✅ Reveals rather than instructs — names what's running underneath summer behaviors
- ✅ Names inner experience — "Am I somebody here?" given explicit language throughout
- ✅ Preserves belonging — boredom honored, clingy behavior reframed as attachment working correctly
- ✅ Reduces pressure — "you don't have to be perfect at this"
- ✅ Tired mom at 10pm test — Leo on the back steps, Emma's rock store — real and immediate
- ✅ Free of AI language — no delve, crucial, robust, comprehensive, leverage, landscape
- ✅ Short paragraphs, real moments, emotional truth throughout
