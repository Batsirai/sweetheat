### LinkedIn Post

Here's something developmental psychologists have known for decades that most parenting conversations don't talk about directly:

By age four or five, the majority of children have formed a "working model" of attachment — a belief about whether love is something they *have* or something they *earn.*

This belief — formed before they can articulate it — predicts their resilience, their risk-taking, their friendship patterns, and how they handle failure well into adulthood.

The good news: working models aren't fixed. They're updated.

The mechanism for updating them is language. Specific, repeated, identity-based language.

Not "I'm so proud of how hard you worked."
Not "you were so good today."

But: *"You are already loved. Not because of today. Because you're you."*

This is the whole thesis behind AlreadyLoved — that what you say to a child in the quiet moments becomes the voice they carry inside themselves when you're not in the room.

Identity precedes behavior. Belonging is the foundation, not the reward.

What you say today becomes their inner voice tomorrow.
